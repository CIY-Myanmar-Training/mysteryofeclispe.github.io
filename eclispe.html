What Is An Eclispe?

An eclipse is when one celestial body moves into the shadow of another.

There are two main types of eclispe:

  1. Solar Eclispe
  2. Lunar Eclispe

Imagine playing hide and seed with sun, earth and moon.

Solar System

The Solar System is the Sun and all the objects that orbit around it. The Sun is orbited by planets, asteroids, comets and other things.

  1. Solar Eclispe

    When the Moon comes between the Sun and Earth, casting its shadow on Earth.

  2. Lunar Eclispe

    When the Earth comes between the Sun and the Moon, causing the Earth's shadow to fall on the Moon.

Why Do Eclipse Happen?

Eclipses happen when the Sun, Moon, and Earth orbit. Sometimes, the Moon moves between the Sun and the Earth, blocking the Sun's light,
and that's called a Solar Eclipse. Other times, the Earth comes between the Sun and the Moon, casting a shadow on the Moon, and that's a Lunar Eclipse. It's like the Sun,
Moon, and Earth are taking turns hiding behind each other in the sky
